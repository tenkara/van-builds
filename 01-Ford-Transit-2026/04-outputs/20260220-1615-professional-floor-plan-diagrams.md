## DIAGRAM NOTES & USAGE INSTRUCTIONS

### For Professional CAD Conversion:

These ASCII diagrams are designed to be easily converted to professional CAD drawings using software like:
- **AutoCAD** (industry standard)
- **SketchUp** (3D visualization, free version available)
- **Fusion 360** (parametric CAD, free for hobbyists)
- **LibreCAD** (free, open-source 2D CAD)

### Recommended Conversion Process:

1. **Import dimensions** from diagrams into CAD software
2. **Draw floor plan** at 1:24 scale (1/2" = 1'-0") for printing
3. **Create elevations** (front, rear, port, starboard) at same scale
4. **Add 3D model** using Transit template (from 03-inputs folder)
5. **Render visualizations** with materials (white walls, LVP floor, etc.)

### Print Recommendations:

- **Full floor plan**: 24"×36" paper (architectural D-size)
- **Individual zones**: 11"×17" paper (tabloid)
- **Elevations**: 11"×17" paper
- **Details**: 8.5"×11" paper (letter)

### Color Coding Suggestions:

When converting to CAD, use these colors for clarity:
- **Walls/Structure**: Black or dark grey
- **Cabinetry**: Brown or tan
- **Appliances**: Light blue
- **Mattress/Cushions**: Light green
- **Water areas**: Blue
- **Electrical**: Red
- **Dimensions**: Dark blue
- **Notes**: Dark grey
