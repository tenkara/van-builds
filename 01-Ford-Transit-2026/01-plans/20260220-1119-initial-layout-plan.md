# Ford Transit 148" WB High Roof Extended - Camper Van Conversion Layout Plan

**Date:** February 20, 2026
**Vehicle:** 2025 Ford Transit 148" Wheelbase, High Roof, Extended Length
**Design Inspiration:** Coastline Vans Cosy Road 170 (adapted from Mercedes Sprinter 170")
**Design Goals:** Maximize window views, east-west bed, full kitchen, wet bath, front seating area

---

## VEHICLE SPECIFICATIONS

### Ford Transit 148" WB ELWB High Roof
- **Wheelbase:** 148" (3,750mm)
- **Cargo Length (approx):** 144-146"
- **Cargo Width:** 70" between wheel wells
- **Interior Height:** 72-76" (high roof)
- **GVWR:** 9,250-9,950 lbs (depending on engine)
- **Window Configuration:** Available with cargo area windows on both sides and rear doors

### Design Challenge: Sprinter 170" → Transit 148"
- **Length Difference:** ~22" shorter
- **Width:** Similar (~70-72" usable)
- **Height:** Transit high roof comparable to Sprinter
- **Solution:** Compress galley length, optimize bed placement, use vertical space

## LAYOUT ZONES (Rear to Front)

### ZONE 1: REAR - East-West Bed (Aft Section)
**Location:** Rear 60-65" of cargo area
**Dimensions:**
- **Bed Size:** 60" wide × 75" long (Queen equivalent)
- **Orientation:** East-West (side-to-side across van width)
- **Height from floor:** 24-30" (maximize under-bed storage)
- **Headroom above bed:** 45-50"

**Features:**
- Under-bed storage: garage-style access, 24-28" height clearance
- Storage for outdoor gear, water tanks, electrical systems
- Bed platform constructed with removable slats for access
- LED strip lighting under bed perimeter
- Reading lights mounted on both sides
- 12V outlets and USB ports on both sides of bed

**Window Integration:**
- Rear door windows provide natural light
- Side cargo windows at head of bed (one on each side)
- No overhead cabinets blocking windows

**Location:** Port (driver) side, approximately 36-40" section
**Dimensions:** 36" long × 26" wide × 76" tall

**Option A: Compact Wet Bath**

### ZONE 3: GALLEY KITCHEN (Starboard Side + Partial Mid)
**Location:** Starboard (passenger) side, approximately 50-55" section
**Dimensions:** 50-55" long × 20-24" deep

**Appliances & Features:**

**Cooking:**
- **Primary:** Countertop space for portable induction cooktop OR instant pot
- **Secondary:** Exterior propane connection for outdoor cooking

**Refrigeration:**
- **12V Compressor Fridge/Freezer:** 65-85L capacity
  - Examples: Dometic CFX3 75DZ, ARB Elements 63Qt

  - Top-loading or front-opening (depends on space)

**Sink & Water:**

- Foot pump or 12V water pump
- Fresh water tank: 20-25 gallons (under bed or in galley base)
- Grey water tank: 20-25 gallons (under van)

**Counter Space:**
- Butcher block or solid surface countertop
- 24-30" of clear counter workspace
- Removable cutting board extensions
- Backsplash to protect wall

**Storage:**
- Upper cabinets: Open shelving or glass-front (maintain window views)
- Lower cabinets: Deep drawers for pots, pans, dishes
- Spice rack and utensil storage
- Overhead mesh netting or small cubbies (below window line)

**Power:**
- 120V AC outlets (2-3) for appliances
- 12V outlets (2) for USB charging, fans
- Main electrical panel accessible in kitchen base cabinet

## WINDOW STRATEGY - "FULL 360 PANORAMIC VIEW"

### Key Design Principle: NO TOP-TO-BOTTOM VIEW BLOCKING

**Window Locations (Ford Transit ELWB):**
1. **Sliding door side (port):** 2 cargo windows
2. **Fixed side (starboard):** 2 cargo windows
3. **Rear doors:** 2 windows (barn doors)
4. **Cab:** Factory windshield, driver/passenger windows

**Design Rules:**
- ✅ **Lower cabinets only:** All storage below window sill line (~36-42" from floor)
- ✅ **Open upper shelving:** Minimal or transparent if above counter
- ✅ **No solid wall cabinets:** Use mesh, netting, or glass-front cabinets only
- ✅ **Bed platform:** Low enough to not block rear windows (max 30" high)
- ✅ **Wet bath:** Frosted window film for privacy, but maintain light transmission

**Window Treatments:**
- Reflectix or insulated covers for temperature control (removable)
- Magnetic or snap-on blackout curtains for sleeping
- Sheer curtains for daytime privacy with light

## ELECTRICAL SYSTEM (Preliminary)

### Power Requirements:
- **Induction cooktop:** 1500-1800W (120V AC)
- **Microwave/convection/air fryer combo:** 1500-2000W (120V AC)
- **Refrigerator:** 45-60W (12V DC)
- **LED Lighting:** 20-40W total (12V DC)
- **Fans, water pump, USB:** 50-100W (12V DC)
- **Misc devices:** 200-400W

### System Components:
- **Lithium Battery Bank:** 400-600Ah (12V) recommended
  - Examples: Battle Born, Victron, RELiON
- **Solar Panels:** 400-800W roof-mounted
- **Inverter/Charger:** 2000-3000W pure sine wave
  - Examples: Victron MultiPlus, Renogy
- **Shore Power Connection:** 30A inlet
- **DC-DC Charger:** 50-60A (charges from alternator while driving)
- **Battery Monitor:** Victron BMV or similar

**Location:** Under bed storage area, accessible via removable panels

---

## PLUMBING SYSTEM (Preliminary)

### Fresh Water:
- **Tank:** 20-25 gallons
- **Pump:** 12V demand pump (3.5-5.0 GPM)
- **Location:** Under galley or under bed
- **Fill:** External gravity fill + internal fill option

### Grey Water:
- **Tank:** 20-25 gallons
- **Location:** Under van chassis (requires professional install)
- **Drain:** Gravity drain valve

### Toilet:
- **Type:** Cassette dry flush (Thetford or Laveo)
- **No black water tank required**
- **Cassette capacity:** 5 gallons
- **External access for dumping**

---

## INSULATION & CLIMATE CONTROL

### Insulation:
- **Walls:** Spray foam or rigid foam board (R-value 10-13)
- **Ceiling:** Spray foam or Thinsulate (R-value 13-19)
- **Floor:** Rigid foam + subfloor (R-value 7-10)
- **Windows:** Reflectix or insulated covers

### Heating:
- **Diesel heater:** 2-5kW (Espar, Webasto, or Chinese diesel heater)
- **Ducted to bed, bathroom, and living areas**
- **Thermostat control**

### Cooling:
- **Maxxair roof fan:** 2 units (one over bed, one over galley)
- **Optional:** 12V portable fan or small AC unit (requires significant power)

### Ventilation:
- Roof fans (intake/exhaust)
- Window vents
- Wet bath dedicated exhaust fan

---

## STORAGE BREAKDOWN

### Under-Bed Storage:
- **Volume:** ~50-60 cubic feet
- **Access:** Side doors or lift-up bed platform
- **Contents:** Outdoor gear, tools, water tanks, electrical components

### Kitchen Storage:
- **Upper:** Open shelves, small cabinets (15-20 cubic feet)
- **Lower:** Drawers and cabinets (20-25 cubic feet)
- **Contents:** Food, cookware, utensils, cleaning supplies

### Wet Bath Storage:
- **Volume:** 3-5 cubic feet
- **Contents:** Toiletries, towels, cleaning supplies

### Overhead Storage:
- **Minimal:** Small mesh nets or cubbies only (maintain window views)
- **Volume:** 5-8 cubic feet
- **Contents:** Lightweight items, books, charging cables

### Garage/Pass-Through Storage:
- **Under bed:** Full width access
- **Contents:** Bikes, outdoor chairs, water sports equipment
